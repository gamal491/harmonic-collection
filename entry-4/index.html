Moon Phases

A Waning Crescent (Hover me!)


There are eight primary phases of the Moon:

  1. New Moon

  2. Waxing Crescent

  3. First Quarter

    4. One of the primary phases in the lunar cycle, occurring about 7 days after the New Moon phase. Exactly half of the Moon's visible side is illuminated by the Sun.
    This lunar phase is symbolic of growth, progession, and renewal. It is known to be a time for meditation, ritual, setting intentions, or as a reminder to start taking steps toward one's goals and dreams.

  4. Waxing Gibbous

  5. Full Moon

  6. Waning Gibbous

    7. The phase after the full moon, when the illuminated portion of the Moon begins to decrease. This phase is larger than a half-moon but not fully round.
    It is a time to look inwards and re-evaluate your goals. It's a time to feel grateful for what has been this month and re-adjust your intentions moving forward.

  7. Third Quarter

  8. Waning Crescent:

    9. Waning means the moon is getting smaller.

    The final phase of the lunar cycle. The waning crescent is just before the New Moon, and is characterised by being a thin crescent.
    It brings a sense of surrender and reflection. It is a time to reflect on your thoughts and experiences and prepare for a fresh start once the new moon is reached.
